## First 14 Days

### Days 1-2: Alphabet, Sound, Greetings

Goal: pronounce Swedish letters, greet someone, give your name, country, and languages.

Study:
- Textbook chapter 1
- Workbook chapter 1
- Pronunciation reference: alphabet and sentence stress

Core phrases:

| English | Swedish |
|---|---|
| Hi | Hej |
| What is your name? | Vad heter du? |
| My name is ... | Jag heter ... |
| Where are you from? | Varifrån kommer du? |
| I come from ... | Jag kommer från ... |
| What languages do you speak? | Vad talar du för språk? |
| I speak English and a little Swedish. | Jag talar engelska och lite svenska. |
| Nice to meet you. | Trevligt att träffas. |
| I understand a little. | Jag förstår lite. |

Mini task:
Write and say five sentences:

```text
Hej! Jag heter ______.
Jag kommer från ______.
Jag talar engelska och lite svenska.
Jag studerar svenska.
Trevligt att träffas!
```

### Days 3-4: Word Order and Questions

Goal: understand the basic Swedish sentence pattern.

Swedish main clauses usually put the verb in position 2.

| Pattern | Swedish | English |
|---|---|---|
| Subject + verb | Jag heter Rony. | My name is Rony. |
| Question word + verb + subject | Vad heter du? | What is your name? |
| Yes/no question: verb + subject | Talar du svenska? | Do you speak Swedish? |

Key question words:

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| vad | what |
| var | where |
| varifrån | from where |
| vem | who |
| hur | how |
| när | when |
| varför | why |

Practice:
- Turn each statement into a question:
  - Du heter Anna. -> Vad heter du?
  - Du kommer från Indien. -> Varifrån kommer du?
  - Du talar engelska. -> Talar du engelska?

### Days 7-8: Nouns and Articles

Swedish nouns use two main genders: common gender `en` and neuter `ett`.

| Indefinite | Definite | English |
|---|---|---|
| en bok | boken | a book / the book |
| en dator | datorn | a computer / the computer |
| ett språk | språket | a language / the language |
| ett land | landet | a country / the country |

Rule of thumb:
- `en` words often take `-en` in the definite form.
- `ett` words often take `-et` in the definite form.
- Learn each noun with its article: `en bok`, not just `bok`.

### Days 13-14: Free Talk Review

Prepare a 60-second self-introduction:

```text
Hej! Jag heter ...
Jag kommer från ...
Nu bor jag i ...
Jag talar ...
Jag studerar svenska eftersom ...
På fritiden gillar jag ...
Tack!
```

Record yourself, listen once, and repeat more slowly. Do not chase perfection yet. Clear rhythm beats perfect pronunciation at the start.

## Pronunciation Notes

Swedish has 29 letters: A-Z plus Å, Ä, Ö. Those last three are separate letters and come at the end of the alphabet.

Approximate letter names:

| Letter | Approx sound cue |
|---|---|
| A | like Swedish long `a`, open "ah" |
| E | like "eh" but long/clear |
| I | like "ee" |
| O | often like "oo" in letter name |
| U | rounded, tighter than English "oo" |
| Y | rounded "ee"; say "ee" with rounded lips |
| Å | like "awe" / "o" |
| Ä | like "air" without strong r |
| Ö | like German/French rounded vowel; say "e" with rounded lips |

Important beginner habit:
- Swedish spelling often marks vowel length. A vowel is usually long before one consonant and short before two or more consonants.
- Example pattern: `tak` has a long vowel, `tack` has a short vowel.

## Weekly Study Routine

Use this 45-minute session:

1. 5 minutes: alphabet or sound warm-up.
2. 10 minutes: read one textbook page aloud.
3. 10 minutes: write your own version of the model dialogue.
4. 10 minutes: do workbook exercises.
5. 5 minutes: quiz yourself with flashcards.
6. 5 minutes: record and replay your voice.
